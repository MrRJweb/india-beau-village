about

A volume kitchen, and proud of it.

Imagine being able to choose between fifteen lamb dishes, twenty chicken, nine seafood and fifteen vegetarian. That isn't flyer talk: it's the board, counted dish by dish, with its prices on display. You could eat at India Beau Village every week of the year without ordering the same thing twice.

We're in Parc-Extension, on Jarry West, a few minutes from Acadie métro. The neighbourhood has been eating here for years: families after school, work crews after their shift, sweet tooths who come just for the mango lassi and the gulab jamun. The kitchen is fully halal, vegetarian takes up a quarter of the board, and the door stays open every day from 11 am to midnight.

No white tablecloths, no staging — big pots, a clay oven, and prices that keep people coming back.

groups & events

A hundred seats, one big hall.

Birthdays, work teams, extended families: the dining room seats up to 100, and a big hall is available for parties. For events elsewhere, the catering service runs off the same board — same dishes, same honest prices, in tray format.
